Internet Privacy

How Privacy Risks Impact Young-Adults

Now more than ever, a large amount of people, namely young-adults, spend a good portion of their time on the Internet. Would you be comfortable with knowing that everything you search for, look at, and send is being seen by other people? Personally, I would have to say no. That being said, did you know that the government collects all of this data?
Privacy, nowadays, is a relative thing, and as so, we must learn safe internet browsing methods to protect ourselves online. Not only is our data at risk, but potentially so can our liberty, there can be a lot of risk when browsing the internet, and it is our own responsibility to know how to stay safe online. It is important to know that you do have a certain level of control over your own privacy, but the only way to exercise it, is to devote time to practice habits we will cover in upcoming pages.

Our Privacy is Deeply at Risk

It is clear that our privacy is deeply at risk because of the ways that we utilize the internet today. When we are using social media, shopping online, accepting cookies of a myriad of different sites, all of the browsers we use are tracking us and using whatever information they can gain from us to make profits off of our metadata. As consumers, we should be more aware of the fact we are being monitored, where our data is going, and what information is being taken of us. It may not be possible to ditch social media for good, or totally go off the grid, but we still have some power to take our digital security into our own hands.

Be Informed!

Informing yourself of issues like this is the first step of learning to protect yourself online, and keep what you want to keep, private. So keep reading, and stay safe while browsing!
