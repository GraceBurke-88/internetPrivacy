Tracking and Security

How are you being tracked in the web? | Online Tracking Explained:

To begin to answer this question, it is important to understand what ‘data’ is, and how it is collected. Data, or information, is collected using “cookies”, which are tiny files placed on your computer through your browser. There are different kinds of cookies, which are used to perform, and collect, different tasks. Session cookies allow you to move through pages without having to keep inputting data. Persistent cookies stay on your computer, track your browsing history, which allow ads from one site (i.e: Wayfair) to follow you. With this data collected, a profile on both the users browsing habits, and of even the user themselves, can be created. These profiles can be bought and sold, sometimes with the idea of targeting ads to the user.

Browser Fingerprinting

In a basic sense, browser fingerprinting can interpret the user's gender, age, current address, ISP provider, sexual orientation, purchasing behaviors, amongst other things. Unless people learn how to protect themselves while browsing all of this arguably personal information, it is potentially out there, exposed. Without this protection, it’s virtually impossible to avoid being tracked unless internet usage is heavily limited.

Basic Practices:

Some very simple and easy ways to start protecting yourself online are clearing search history and site cookies, using a private browser, paying attention to permission requests, checking app permissions, stopping and thinking about giving away data like age, address, and name. At the very basic level, these practices will protect a user from lower level site tracking.
