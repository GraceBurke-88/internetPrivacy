Tracking and Security

How you are being tracked in the web? | Online Tracking Explained:
Data is collected using “cookies”, which are tiny files placed on your computer through your browser. There are different kinds of cookies, which are used to perform, and collect, different tasks. Session cookies allow you to move through pages without having to keep inputting data. Persistent cookies stay on your computer, track your browsing history, which allow ads from one site (i.e: Wayfair) to follow you.

Browser Fingerprinting
In a basic sense, browser fingerprinting establishes the user's gender, age, current address, ISP provider, and can even describe sexual orientation, purchasing behaviors, among other things. Nowadays, it’s virtually impossible to avoid being tracked unless you heavily limit your internet usage.

Basic Practices:
Clear history and cookies, use a private browser, pay attention to permission requests, check app permissions, stop and think about giving away data like your age, address, and name.
