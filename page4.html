Basic Security

How can you avoid being constantly watched by these sites?

Luckily, there are different levels of precautions you can take to ensure you aren't being traced by the sites you normally use.
A basic precautions that is very powerful is:

VPNs

Using a VPN masks an IP address by creating a private address from a public one. This way, it makes it exponentially difficult for a user's data to be collected, and overrides any restrictions usually placed on local sites. Therefore, when a website, an ISP provider, or anyone else looking into the traffic your device is creating, they cannot pinpoint what websites you are visiting, eliminates tracking cookies, state or country restrictions, and protects information such as card numbers, and personal information. VPNs are fairly popular these days, and there are even apps that enable VPN use on mobile phones, which thinking about how often you are on your phone, and how much you do on it, is worth looking into. Some of the most famous VPN services available include: nordVPN, and expressVPN.
Link to tutorial: How to set up a VPN

Basic security measures you can take to leave a smaller footprint.

There are other basic security measures you can take to leave a smaller footprint.
Consider :
Using secure browsers like Google Chrome or Microsoft Edge. A good way to know if you are using a secure website is seeing if there is a lock next to the website URL.
Learn to keep antivirus software up to date, and regularly scan your device to ensure no malware is installed erroneously. Antivirus software also warns of suspicious websites, and blocks spontaneous file downloads.
Deleting site ‘cookies’ periodically ensures a certain site cannot track you for a long period of time, or consider disabling them completely.
